Welcome to the Neurofloristry Lab's study on...

Flower Power: investigating the aesthetics of flowers


Here you can find information about the survey. To complete the survey please indicate your consent to participate at the bottom of the page.


You are invited to participate in a research study being conducted by:
  • Associate Professor Tamara Watson (Western Sydney University, School of Social Sciences and Psychology),
  • Dr Matthew Patten (Western Sydney University, School of Social Sciences and Psychology),
  • Professor Branka Spehar (UNSW Sydney, School of Psychology), and
  • Dr Damien Mannion (UNSW Sydney, School of Psychology).
    •
This project aims to establish why we find certain types of natural stimuli pleasant to look at.

How is the study being paid for?
This project is funded by an Australian Research Council Discovery Project Grant DP170101537, 'Flower Power: Natural Form, Aesthetics and the Human Brain' awarded to A/Prof. Tamara Watson, Prof. Branka Spehar, Dr Damien Mannion.

What will I be asked to do?
Participants will be presented with images of flowers, or other natural stimuli like fish or landscapes, and may be asked how appealing they find the image or to provide a judgement about a defined aspect of the appearance of the stimulus (e.g. the dominant colour or a description of the shape, etc.). Participants will also be asked to estimate their design and floristry experience.
Participants' responses will be combined with computer generated measurements of the stimuli and classification algorithms will be used to determine which aspects of the stimuli presented determine which stimuli are the most reliably rated as most attractive.

How much of my time will I need to give?
You will be asked to complete a 20-minute experimental session online.

What benefits will I, and/or the broader community, receive for participating?
There are no direct benefits to you or your community for participating in this research.

Will the study involve any risk or discomfort for me? If so, what will be done to rectify it?
The study will not involve any discomfort for you. The study will not involve any unpleasant images and any decision you are asked to make about the stimuli will be restricted to how attractive you find them or to analysis of a defined aspect of the appearance of the image.

How do you intend to publish or disseminate the results?
It is anticipated that the results of this research project will be published and/or presented in a variety of forums including in academic journal articles, conference presentations and on the lab website. In any publication and/or presentation, information will be provided in such a way that the participant cannot be identified. You will not be identifiable by the data collected and as such your responses will be anonymous.

Will the data and information that I have provided be disposed of?
No. Your data will be used as per Western Sydney University's Open Access Policy. This means that data collected from this study can be made available online and world-wide in perpetuity. You will not be identifiable by the data collected.

Can I withdraw from the study?
Participation is entirely voluntary and you are not obliged to be involved. If you do participate you can withdraw at any time without giving reason.
Any data you have supplied during an online, remotely conducted experiment will be retained because your data is collected anonymously and cannot be identified. Should you not complete the online study your data will be deleted.

Can I tell other people about the study?
Yes, you can tell other people about the study providing them with this link: http://watsonvisionlab.org
